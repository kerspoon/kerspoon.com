Web Typography

Body Text

Point size: in print, the optimal point size for body text is 10-12 point. On the web, the optimal size is 15-25 pixels.

Line length: aim for an average line length of 45-90 characters, including spaces. Alternatively, use the alphabet test to set line length. If you need the body text to fit a broad range of widths, then use adjustments to point size and page margins to keep the line length in the right range. You should be able to fit between two and three alphabets on your line, like so:

abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyzabcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyzabcdefgh

Line spacing: for most text, the optimal line spacing is between 120% and 145% of the point size. Use the line-height property.

Font: Go to Google Fonts and find a sans-serif for titles and a serirf one for body text. Here I use PT Sans and Arvo.

Headings

Writers often use too many levels of headings. Headings that announce every topic, subtopic, minitopic, and microtopic are exhausting. Readers should be able to understand the framework of your argument just by reading the headings.

Use bold, not italic. You can make the point size bigger, but just a little. The best way to emphasize a heading is by putting space above and below
